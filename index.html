Why Strong Passwords Matter

Learn about password security and how to protect yourself from data breaches.

  • Strong passwords are your first line of defense against hackers, data breaches, and identity theft. Weak passwords can be cracked in seconds, while strong passwords can take years or even decades to break, even with powerful computers.

  • A strong password should be at least 12 characters long and include a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special symbols. It should avoid common words, personal information, and predictable patterns.

  • Data breaches occur when hackers gain unauthorized access to databases containing user information. This can happen through weak passwords, phishing attacks, software vulnerabilities, or insider threats. Once breached, hackers can access accounts, steal personal information, or sell credentials on the dark web.

  • Absolutely! Using the same password across multiple accounts is extremely risky. If one account is compromised, hackers can access all your other accounts. Each account should have a unique, strong password to minimize the impact of a potential breach.

  • It's recommended to change passwords every 3-6 months, especially for important accounts like banking, email, and work accounts. However, if you suspect a breach or receive a security notification, change your password immediately.
